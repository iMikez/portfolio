Insights

What makes you stand out from other Shopify developers?

My QA background means I don’t just write functional code — I write testable, durable, user-centric code. I think about edge cases, performance bottlenecks, and long-term scalability while delivering elegant frontend solutions.

How do you approach building custom Shopify sections?

I start by understanding the purpose behind the section — is it conversion-focused, editorial, or brand-enhancing? Then I design a reusable, schema-powered section that empowers content teams to control layout, imagery, and behavior. Every section I build is cleanly scoped, testable, and responsive across breakpoints.

Can you describe your QA experience and how it applies to development?

I’ve worked with manual and automated testing (including Selenium, Cypress, and Postman), led test case planning, and caught critical bugs in pre-release cycles. This experience taught me how to debug fast, plan for risk, and validate user behavior — all of which make my development more informed and stable.

What’s your process for translating Figma into functional sections?

I dissect Figma files layer by layer, noting layout behavior, font scaling, spacing logic, and interactive elements. Then I reproduce the UI using semantic HTML and CSS, aligning pixel-perfection with performance. I maintain open comms with designers to clarify intent and suggest scalable improvements where needed.

How do you handle cross-browser/device compatibility in your work?

I build mobile-first and test across key breakpoints and devices. My QA mindset ensures I check for layout shifts, tap targets, accessibility, and browser-specific quirks.

Do you write tests as a developer?

Yes — I incorporate testing into my dev flow, including unit tests where appropriate and manual QA passes for all interactive components. I also write reusable, test-friendly code with QA automation in mind.

What kind of projects are you most excited by?

Projects where performance, user experience, and branding meet. I love working on interactive custom sections, advanced filtering, and anything that challenges me to blend creativity with technical depth.

What performance optimisations have you implemented on Shopify?

I optimise by lazy-loading assets, deferring non-critical scripts, removing unused CSS, and compressing images through Shopify's built-in tools and custom logic. I also reduce Liquid loops, cache complex logic using `assign`, and minimise DOM reflows to ensure smooth interactions and fast load times.
